I Always Check My Glucose Summary First. Here's Why.

When you're living with Type 1 diabetes like me, every number matters. But it's not just about one reading — it’s about the whole picture. That’s where the Glucose Summary in Glucostats really helps.

It shows me my average glucose, the highest and lowest values, and how much time I spent in range. I can see right away if I’ve had too many highs, or if I was low during the night.

There’s even a colored chart that makes it super easy to understand — green is good, red means I need to be careful.

Sometimes I scroll through the report and think, “Wow, I did better this week!” Other times I notice things I didn’t realize — like how I kept going high after lunch. That helps me and my parents make smarter decisions, like adjusting insulin or changing what I eat.

I don't just see numbers — I see patterns. And that makes a big difference.

Track Your Insulin Usage

When I started using insulin, it felt like I was just guessing how much to take. Sometimes I needed more, sometimes less — and figuring out why was really hard.

That's why I like using Glucostats to track both my bolus (mealtime) and basal (long-acting) insulin. It shows me how much I took each day in easy bar charts, so I can spot changes and trends right away.

If I've been going low in the afternoons, I can look back and see, "Oh — maybe I had too much bolus at lunch." Or if I've been waking up high, it might mean I need to adjust my basal insulin a little. The app helps me and my parents figure it out together.

Before, insulin just felt like numbers. Now it makes sense.

Glucostats doesn't just help me track — it helps me learn what my body needs.
